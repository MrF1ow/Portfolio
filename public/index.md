## About

### My Faith through Life

Everyone's faith journey looks different. Mine took a few detours before it stuck.

Growing up, faith wasn't really a thing in our house. We'd go to church here and there, but nothing consistent. Then when I was eight, my parents got divorced. We left my dad, moved into a rental, and everyone dealt with it the way people deal with hard stuff — imperfectly.

A few years after the split, my mom found her footing in Christ. She got serious about her faith and tried to bring us kids along for the ride. But if you've ever been a teenager, you know how that goes. My sister did her thing. My brother did his. I actually tried — went to church with my mom and got baptized at 13.

Then 15 hit. My body changed, I got athletic, and suddenly girls were paying attention. I'm extroverted by nature, so I ran with it. Phone numbers, Snapchats, all of it. You can imagine how much room that left for faith. Not a lot.

But somewhere in high school, I met this girl. Had no idea she'd end up being my wife. I just knew I was in love. Junior year I asked her out, and we've been together ever since.

It wasn't until we got engaged that we both made a real decision — faith comes first, not the values the world tries to sell you. That year, together and on our own, we grew in ways I can't fully put into words.

Since then, my faith has only gotten stronger. I've moved a lot, switched careers, changed scenery more times than I can count. Every single one of those shifts brought me closer to the Lord. I strive every day to live the way He would and to share that with whoever will listen.

### The Degree on the Wall

I do have a degree. But getting there was more of an adventure than a straight line.

I started in a Spanish immersion school. Went all the way through middle school in it too. Then I quit because I realized I don't actually like speaking Spanish. Do I regret it? A little. Can I speak Spanish now? Not even close.

High school was International Baccalaureate — which is basically AP for people who think they're fancy. It was brutal, honestly harder than some of my college classes. But we got through it.

After graduation, I went straight to college thinking I'd go into healthcare. Physical therapist or surgeon — I couldn't decide. Health and fitness had become a huge part of my life at 15, so it felt like a natural fit. Then I got a job as a physical therapy aide and reality hit. There's a lot I love about healthcare, but way too much I don't. It's a hobby for me, not a career.

The other thing I'd always loved was software and technology. So I took one of those free online courses from Harvard over a summer. I was hooked. Two years into college, I switched my major. Luckily most of those first two years were gen ed, so it didn't set me back too far.

The shift in thinking took some adjusting. Algorithmic problem-solving is a different beast than standard STEM coursework. But once it clicked, it clicked hard. I realized that with just a laptop and a cup of coffee, the number of problems I can solve for people is basically infinite. That's what drives me.

Now I have a B.S. in Computer Science from Oregon State University. And I'm still trying to learn something new every single day.

### I Have Been Blessed

We all got family. Mine's pretty frickin' crazy. Smart and efficient, but absolutely nuts. If you ever meet them, you'll know exactly what I'm talking about.

I'm the youngest of 15 cousins. Yeah, 15. We're not going into the full family tree because honestly I'm not sure I can name all of them. So let's stick to the immediate crew.

I have a brother and a sister. My sister is awesome. My brother? Even cooler. Don't tell her I said that.

My mom is truly the best mom anybody could have, and I'm not afraid to say it — I'm a mama's boy. I call her a couple times a week and love every conversation. She's one of my best friends.

Then there's my wife. She's everything. I get to wake up next to a gorgeous woman every morning and I genuinely don't deserve it. She can also bake like nobody's business. I still haven't found a place that beats her cinnamon rolls. It's insane.

There's a reason I gained 20 pounds over COVID, and it's because I was at her place constantly. I ate so good. So good. I don't regret a single bite.

After my faith, family is the most important thing in my life. I don't know what I'd do without them.

### Outside of Software

Don't get me wrong — I love waking up, pouring a cup of coffee, putting the headphones on, and just going at it. But software isn't the only thing that gets me out of bed.

I was born in Oregon. That is the definition of outdoors. Hiking, exploring new places, meeting new people — it's all second nature. I'm always down to try something new. Bungee jumping? Okay, maybe not bungee jumping. But most things.

And then there's food. I'm not judging anybody here, but if you say you eat to live, I'm so sorry. I live to eat. Are you kidding me? Food is fantastic. Asian cuisine, Italian, Greek — I love it all and I'll try almost anything.

Almost. I've had a cricket. That's about where I draw the line. A beetle? Maybe if you paid me enough. But I honestly have no idea what that number would be.

Beyond all of that, I've been blessed with an incredible life. Ups and downs, sure — but overall? Amazing. I have a wife who is so far out of my league it's not even funny. My mom is an absolute bada**. My sister flies planes. My brother is incredible. The people in my life are something I don't deserve, but here I am.

I wish this kind of life for anybody.
